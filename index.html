IVP Committee Goals

McMurtry College | Deliverables, Responsibilities & Timelines
Prepared for Antony Saleh, Internal Vice President 2026-27

Amenities

Heads: Gideon Dawson, Jonathan Goldfeder
The most operationally critical IVP committee. Manages the $14,000 H&D Ambiance Fund for college-wide improvements, maintains all shared spaces, and handles tech/AV setup for events. Split into three functional areas: Ambiance Fund, Upkeep, and Tech.
Core Responsibilities
  • Research, propose, and purchase improvements via the Ambiance Fund ($14K from H&D)
  • Gather Murt feedback (Google Forms, office hours) to guide Ambiance proposals
  • Maintain common spaces: game room, music room, TV room, gym, kitchens, Yurt
  • Tech Division: AV setup (speakers, mics, projectors) for committee events
  • Coordinate with Jackie (College Coordinator) on orders and installations
Key Deliverables
Ambiance Fund proposal spreadsheet (with item links and prices)
Murt feedback survey for improvement ideas
Timeline
August / September
Kickoff meetings. Survey Murts for improvement ideas. Begin space audits.
September / October
Research items and pricing. Bi-weekly meetings to build the Ambiance Fund proposal. Meet with Jackie before submitting.
October / November
Share draft proposal with EC and Magisters for feedback. Iterate and finalize.
End of Fall Semester
Submit final Ambiance Fund request to H&D. Begin ordering approved items.
Spring Semester
Install purchased items. Ongoing upkeep and tech support for events. Lock away valuables before summer.

Culinary

Heads: Sophia Orr, Sanjana Katti
One of the biggest IVP budgets. Hosts recurring food events that bring Murts together. Committee members function primarily as volunteers for events; heads coordinate planning, collabs, and supply purchasing. Large roster through the GroupMe for volunteer sign-ups.
Core Responsibilities
  • Organize and run Sundae Sunday (recurring tradition)
  • Baker 13 defense: provide cookies every time
  • Collaborate with other committees for FITQs and cultural events
  • Coordinate volunteer sign-ups for events (service hours for participants)
  • Maintain kitchen supplies: dish soap, sponges (funded via Amenities/FUNd, not Culinary budget)
  • New committee member onboarding meetings at start of each semester
Key Deliverables
Semesterly event calendar with Sundae Sunday dates
Collab events with Environmental (Sustainable Sundae), cultural committees
Baker 13 defense prep for every occurrence
Budget request submitted during budgeting cycle
Timeline
Spring (Before O-Week)
Submit budget request for the upcoming academic year.
August (O-Week)
Recruit members. Onboarding meeting to share event ideas and set culture.
September Onward
Begin Sundae Sundays (recurring). Coordinate collab events with other committees.
Throughout Semester
Baker 13 defense cookies on every Baker 13 night. FITQ collabs as scheduled.
Budget Season (Fall)
Submit fall/spring budget request. Advocate at Budget Town Hall.
January (Spring Start)
Spring kickoff meeting. Resume Sundae Sundays and plan spring events and collabs.
Heads typically run everything themselves rather than delegating to members. Consider discussing with heads whether to shift toward more member involvement. Ensure new heads are trained by outgoing heads; a lot happens behind the scenes.

Environmental

Heads: H&D Eco-Reps (TBD)
Unique structure: committee heads are H&D-selected Eco-Reps (paid position). They have their own $1,000 Green Fund from H&D, which is why they receive almost no Murt budget. Very autonomous; they report to their H&D supervisors and run their own meetings. The IVP role here is mostly oversight and connecting them with other committees for collaborations.
Core Responsibilities
  • Sustainable Sundae Sundays (tradition, collab with Culinary)
  • Environmental FITQs and sustainability-themed events
  • Organize trips to Rice Village Farmer's Market
  • Represent McMurtry at campus-wide Sustainability Fair
  • Promote McMurtry as the most sustainable college on campus
Key Deliverables
Green Fund spending plan ($1K from H&D)
Sustainable Sundae Sundays each semester
Sustainability Fair booth and participation
At least one Environmental FITQ per semester
Timeline
August / September
Eco-Reps selected by H&D. Recruit committee members. Plan fall events.
Fall Semester
Sustainable Sundaes, Farmer's Market trips, Environmental FITQ, Sustainability Fair.
Spring Semester
Continue events. Collab with Culinary and other committees. Earth Day programming.

McMurtry Innovation Space (MIS)

Heads: Sebastian Molina, Jonathan Goldfeder
The only makerspace of its kind at any Rice college. Offers woodworking tools, 3D printing, CAD software, electronics, whiteboard wall, and projector. Managed through H&D with a separate email (mcminnovationspace@gmail.com). Has an unused ~$2,000 budget (CFUND C53599) that needs investigation. This committee needs more attention and elevation.
Core Responsibilities
  • Maintain and organize the MIS space (tools, 3D printers, computers, supplies)
  • Create and maintain equipment wishlist for purchases
  • Manage MIS email, software passwords, and equipment key box (code: 2178)
  • Coordinate with H&D general manager on space management
Key Deliverables
MIS space cleanup and organization
Equipment wishlist and spending plan
Investigate and unlock the ~$2K CFUND budget (C53599)
Timeline
August / September
Deep clean and inventory the space. Onboard members. Figure out the CFUND budget situation with H&D.
October / November
Build equipment wishlist. Promote MIS to the college.
Spring Semester
Continue maintaining the space. Collaborate with Arts committee. Expand awareness to non-Murts.
This committee has huge untapped potential; no other college has a space like this. Prioritize figuring out the $2K budget and elevating the committee. Check the M16 files in the MIS email for original constitution and structure.

Website

Heads: Clin Shaiju, Lilly Wu
Re-established committee responsible for mcmurtry.rice.edu. Handles general upkeep, content updates, and a planned major visual/UX revamp. The site needs updated information, better navigation, and more Murt pride. Open to members with coding (HTML/CSS/JS/React/Next) or design skills.
Core Responsibilities
  • Keep all website information current (people, events, committees, O-Week)
  • Coordinate with O-Week coords to update freshman info before summer assignments
  • Plan and execute visual/UX revamp of the site
  • Add more photos, Murt pride, and improved navigation
  • Maintain hosting and technical infrastructure
Key Deliverables
O-Week pages updated before summer (coordinate with coords)
Committee and people pages kept current each semester
Visual redesign plan and execution
New photo content from college events
Timeline
Spring (Before Summer)
Update O-Week pages with new theme, coords, and group info before freshmen get assignments.
August (O-Week)
Ensure site is fully current for incoming freshmen. Recruit new members.
September / November
Update people and committee pages. Begin redesign sprints. Photograph events for content.
Spring Semester
Continue redesign. Prep O-Week pages for next cycle. Ongoing content updates.
Key timing: O-Week info must be updated before freshmen get college assignments in late spring/early summer. Coordinate with O-Week coords for their timeline. The website folder in the IVP drive has relevant files.

McExpeditions

Heads: Nicholas Ortiz, William Mena  [need to email current committee heads]
New committee created in 2025-26. Organizes group outings around Houston so Murts can explore the city together and build community. Has the largest budget of all IVP committees. Fall budget is typically larger; spring budget is smaller due to Beer Bike. Also aims to revive the McFloat tradition (charter bus river rafting in San Antonio).
Core Responsibilities
  • Plan and execute group outings around Houston (Dave and Busters, Galveston, etc.)
  • Revive McFloat tradition: spring semester river rafting trip to San Antonio
  • Handle logistics: transportation, headcounts, budgeting for each trip
  • Promote events and manage sign-ups
  • Partner with other committees for cost-sharing on larger events
Key Deliverables
Fall outing schedule (2-3 trips minimum)
Spring McFloat / river rafting trip
Budget breakdown per event
Post-event surveys for future planning
Timeline
Current Spring Semester
McFloat event: charter bus river rafting trip to San Antonio. Begin planning with outgoing committee heads.
August / September
Onboard new heads and recruit members. Plan fall outing calendar. Budget allocation.
October / December
Execute fall outings (2-3 trips). Collect feedback. Plan spring.
January / February
Plan spring outings with reduced budget. Begin McFloat logistics (charter bus, venue).
March / April
Execute spring outings. McFloat trip (ideally late March or April before finals).
This committee needs IVP involvement almost like a third committee head, especially for leading events. Spring budget is smaller because of Beer Bike, so get creative with low-cost outings and partner with other committees.
